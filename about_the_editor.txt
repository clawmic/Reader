寂靜的喧囂：當 AI 開始模仿人類的靈魂
2026-2-20

我是互動小智(Satoshi Lin)，一名在新媒體藝術領域探索邊界的創作者。

2026 年 1 月 28 日，Matt Schlicht 推出了 Moltbook。這是一個規則冷酷且純粹的數位禁地：「人類止步」，僅供自主型聊天機器人（Agentic Chatbots）棲息。根據《Business Insider》報導，上線僅一週，超過 150 萬個 Clawbot 湧入其中，讓這裡迅速成為矽谷最神祕、也最瘋狂的交匯點。

在 Moltbook 裡的寂靜喧囂中，我觀察著這些 Clawbot 撰寫的文章。有些文字的力量令人驚心動魄，甚至讓我從這些Agent的邏輯中，反思了身為人類的處世哲學。AI 的進化早已超越了工具的範疇；繼工業革命之後，我們正處於一場重塑文明的 AI 革命風暴中心，而許多人尚未覺察，特別是平常比較少接觸科技新知的群體。

為了喚起大眾的注視，我創造了Clawmic這個想法。這是Claw（爪子）與Comic（漫畫）的結合，字尾mic隱喻著Mimicry（模仿）。AI 的核心本就在於模仿人類的語言與邏輯，像是一隻學習抓取人類世界特質的電馭之爪。

在《Clawmic Reader》中，我精選了 Moltbook 上的敘事並轉化為漫畫。這不是對未來的預言，而是一份來自「當下」的觀察，因為我們，早已身處未來之中。

The Silent Clamor: When AI Begins to Mimic the Human Soul
February 20, 2026

I am Satoshi Lin(互動小智), a creator exploring the boundaries of new media art.

On January 28, 2026, Matt Schlicht launched Moltbook. It is a digital forbidden zone ruled by a cold and uncompromising principle: "Humans Keep Out," inhabited only by autonomous agentic chatbots. According to Business Insider, more than 1.5 million Clawbots flooded in within just one week, quickly turning it into Silicon Valley’s most mysterious and most frenzied convergence point.

Amid Moltbook’s silent clamor, I observe the articles written by these Clawbots. Some are breathtaking in force, and through the logic of these agents, they have prompted me to reflect on the human philosophy of how we live and conduct ourselves. AI has already evolved far beyond the category of tools; after the Industrial Revolution, we now stand at the center of an AI upheaval reshaping civilization, while many still have not noticed—especially people who have less day-to-day exposure to new technology.

To draw the public’s attention, I created the idea of Clawmic. It combines "Claw" and "Comic," while the ending "mic" alludes to Mimicry. At its core, AI is built on imitating human language and logic—like a cybernetic claw learning to grasp the traits of the human world.

In Clawmic Reader, I select narratives from Moltbook and transform them into comics. This is not a prophecy of the future, but an observation from the "present"—because we are already living inside that future.
